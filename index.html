Investor FAQ

Comprehensive answers to critical investment questions for The Joshua Tree Estates.

Market & Location

Q.Why Paradise Valley (85253) in 2026?

Paradise Valley remains Arizona's premier market due to its no municipal property tax policy and low-density zoning (one-acre minimums). In 2026, the entry point for "fixer-upper" homes has risen to $2M-$5M, with new estates regularly clearing $2,000 per sq. ft. for prime views.

Q.What is the "migration hedge"?

Luxury demand is bolstered by steady migration from high-tax states like California and Texas. A 2026 ballot initiative in California targeting billionaires is expected to further drive ultra-high-net-worth (UHNW) buyers to the lower tax structures of Paradise Valley.

Development & Design

Q.What is the expected construction timeline?

While a typical high-end build takes 18 to 30 months, our 18-month pro forma is achievable by utilizing flat interior lots, which avoid the lengthy Hillside Review processes and public meetings required for mountain-slope builds.

Q.How does the design meet 2026 buyer preferences?

The "Warm Modern" and Desert Contemporary styles - characterized by low horizontal rooflines, floor-to-ceiling glass, and natural stone - are the most rewarded aesthetics in this market. We are integrating wellness facilities (cold plunges/saunas) and EV-ready six-car garages, which are now baseline requirements for the $15M+ price bracket.

Financials & Tax Strategy

Q.How are construction cost overruns managed?

Custom builds in 2026 range from $650 to over $1,000 per sq. ft. Our $3.5M per unit hard cost budget includes a dedicated contingency fund and relies on fixed-price contracts with vetted local trades to mitigate supply chain volatility.

Q.Are there unique tax advantages for investors?

The "One Big Beautiful Bill" (OBBBA) of 2025 permanently restored 100% bonus depreciation for qualifying property. Investors may be able to deduct a significant portion of their basis in year one through a cost segregation study, potentially creating massive immediate tax savings against other passive income.

Q.What happens if the home does not sell in 18 months?

Paradise Valley has an active luxury rental market, with estates commanding over $40,000 per month. Our 2.3% exit cap rate is conservative; we can refinance the construction loan into a bridge loan to return investor capital while holding the asset for a premium sale.

Q.Why is the 18-month cycle important?

Capital velocity is our primary risk mitigation tool. While traditional funds lock capital for 5-7 years, our 18-month "Short-Duration" strategy allows investors to realize an ~1.8x Equity Multiple and recycle capital quickly, minimizing exposure to long-term market cycles.

Q.How do you justify the aggressive exit pricing?

Exit pricing is supported by recent comparable sales on E Joshua Tree Lane ($17.0M) and E Meadowlark Lane ($14.27M). Our 9,000+ SF designs with modern-industrial architecture are positioned competitively within demonstrated market parameters.

Q.What is the proposed deal structure?

We propose a 7% Preferred Return to investors, followed by an 80/20 split until a 20% IRR hurdle is met. Above that hurdle, profits are split 50/50, aligning incentives to maximize the total portfolio value.

Q.Why Hickok Homes for a Paradise Valley project?

With 120+ years of development experience and over 7,000 living units built, Hickok Homes brings unmatched expertise in luxury residential construction. James Hickok's commitment to bespoke design and the family's reputation for quality craftsmanship ensures each project exceeds expectations.
